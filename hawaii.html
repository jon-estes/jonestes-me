Research

Choose Your Island

There are four main islands for weddings in Hawaiʻi. Each has a completely different vibe, price point, and logistics profile. Here's the honest breakdown:

🏙️
Oʻahu
The Gathering Place
  • Most affordable flights & hotels
  • Most vendor options / competition
  • Easy logistics for guests
  • Beach packages from $395
  • Widest accommodation range
  • Busy / touristy beaches
  • Up to 6 ceremonies at once near Waikiki
  • Heavy traffic island-wide
  • Less intimate / romantic feel
Budget-friendliest. Ceremony packages $395–$3,300. Flights cheapest from West Coast.
🌺
Maui
The Valley Isle
  • Most romantic atmosphere
  • Stunning beaches (Polo, Fleming, White Rock)
  • Great vendor selection
  • Beach packages from ~$2,550
  • Best of city + nature blend
  • Costs ~2× the Big Island
  • Resort prices are premium
  • Busy tourist spots
  • Post-Lahaina fire some areas still recovering
Mid-range. Beach packages $2,550–$7,250. Average destination wedding ~$9,900.
🌿
Kauaʻi
The Garden Isle
  • Most dramatic / romantic scenery
  • Intimate, quiet, less crowded
  • Napali Coast backdrop
  • Fewer tourists = more privacy
  • Wettest island — high rain risk
  • Fewer vendors = less competition
  • More expensive daily costs
  • Fewer flight options
  • Venue avg $15,000–$35,000
Priciest overall. Avg $15k–$35k. Best for intimate elopements with nature focus.
🌋
Big Island
Hawaiʻi Island
  • Most affordable of the four
  • Least crowded beaches
  • Dramatic lava/volcano backdrop
  • Authentic Hawaiian culture
  • Packages from $995 (photo)
  • Very spread out — long drives
  • Rocky/rough shoreline in spots
  • Less infrastructure
  • Fewer direct flights
  • Active volcanoes (air quality risk)
Most budget-friendly. ~½ the cost of Maui. Great Kona-area beaches.

Budget Breakdown $10–15k

A small beach ceremony (under 25 guests) is very achievable at this budget. Here's a realistic breakdown based on current pricing:

✈️ Flights (2 people, West Coast) $800–$1,200

Legal Requirements

What You Need to Get Married

1

Hawaii Marriage License — $70

Apply online in advance at ehawaii.gov, then meet with a licensed agent in person upon arrival. License is valid for 30 days. Must be obtained before the ceremony.

2

Beach Permit (DLNR Wiki Permit) — $40–$50

All public beach weddings in Hawaiʻi are classified as "commercial activity" and require a Right-of-Entry permit from the Dept. of Land & Natural Resources. Max 25 guests. Apply at ehawaii.gov/permits.

3

Liability Insurance — $75–$150

State requires a $1 million liability policy. One-day "special event" insurance is available from many providers for $75–$150. Must name the State of Hawaii as additional insured.

4

Licensed Officiant

Must be licensed by the State of Hawaiʻi. Most wedding packages include an officiant. You can also register a friend/family member through the state's online system at ehawaii.gov.

5

Beach Rules to Know

No arches, gazebos, chairs, or amplified sound on most public beaches without a special permit. No artificial flowers. "Leave No Trace" required. Max 25 guests at beach ceremonies. No alcohol on beaches.

Smart Planning

Budget-Saving Tips

📅
Book Way Early (You Have Time!)
With 8/20/2028 as your date, you have over 2 years. Affordable vendors get booked first — locking in early saves significantly on photography and ceremony packages.
📆
Choose a Weekday
Monday–Thursday weddings are often significantly cheaper for venues and vendors. Since guests are traveling anyway, a mid-week date makes sense.
🏖️
Go Beach Over Resort
A public beach permit costs $40–$50 vs. resort venue fees of $3,000–$20,000+. A beach ceremony can be more beautiful and intimate anyway.
📸
Hire a Local Photographer
A mainland photographer can add $2,000+ in travel costs. Local Hawaii photographers know the best light, spots, and weather patterns — and cost less.
🍽️
Reception at a Restaurant
Skip the catered venue reception. Book a private lanai or back room at a local restaurant for your group dinner — far more affordable and often more fun.
📦
Use All-in-One Packages
Companies like Simple Maui Wedding, Weddings of Hawaii, and AlohaFunWeddings bundle officiant + photographer + permit + florals. Bundling saves vs. booking separately.
🌧️
Avoid Rainy Season
August is generally dry season in Hawaii — great news for 8/20! The drier west/south shores of each island are your safest bets for outdoor ceremonies.
🏡
Consider Airbnb + Private Property
A private property wedding requires no beach permit, allows arches + chairs, and a well-chosen Airbnb can double as lodging. Can actually save money overall.

Recommendation

Best Fit for Your Budget

Best Budget Pick

🌋 Big Island (Kona Area) — or — 🏙️ Oʻahu

For a $10–15k budget with a small, intimate group, the Big Island's Kona coast is the most affordable choice — about half the cost of Maui, uncrowded beaches, and dramatic scenery. Kukio Beach and Kikaua Point Park are stunning ceremony spots.

If easy flight access for guests (flying from the mainland) is a priority, Oʻahu is the practical pick — the most direct flights, widest hotel range, and ceremony packages starting at $395. Just steer away from the Waikiki corridor for a more intimate feel.

Maui is the most romantic but pushes your budget harder. Kauaʻi is spectacular but priciest overall and has the most rain risk.

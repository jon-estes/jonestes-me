What is a scion?

A scion is a short cutting (4–6 inches, 2–3 buds) taken from a named producing tree. You graft it onto rootstock to grow a true-to-variety tree.

Grafted trees fruit in 1–3 years. Seed-grown take 8–20 years and produce unpredictable fruit.

Why scions — not trees?

Nursery-grown trees are excellent, but they're expensive to ship, heavily regulated across state lines, and limit your rootstock choices. Scionwood is compact, ships well when properly handled, and puts you in control of which rootstock your new tree grows on — which matters enormously for NorCal soil conditions, cold tolerance, and root-rot resistance.

If you're a home grower who already has a seedling rootstock, or you're working with a local nursery on grafts, quality scionwood from a known mother tree is exactly what you need. That's what we sell.

Grafting avocados — an overview

Avocados are grafted rather than grown from seed because seed-grown trees take 8–20 years to produce and yield unpredictable fruit. Grafted trees reproduce the exact genetics of the scion variety and typically produce fruit within 1–3 years of planting.

What you'll need

  • A healthy rootstock seedling (6–18 months old, pencil-diameter stem ideal)
  • A scion cutting — fresh, with 2–3 plump, healthy buds
  • A sharp, sterilized grafting knife or new razor blade
  • Grafting tape or parafilm to seal the union
  • Optional: grafting wax or budding rubber for additional protection

Common graft types for avocado

Cleft graft — Most common for home grafters. Rootstock stem is cut flat and split; scion is cut to a wedge and inserted. Works well when rootstock is thicker than the scion.

Side-veneer graft — Good when scion and rootstock are similar in diameter. A long slanted cut creates maximum cambium contact. Preferred by professional nurseries.

Whip-and-tongue graft — Best for matching diameters. Interlocking tongues create a strong, self-aligning union.

Rootstock for NorCal conditions

For Sacramento Valley conditions, Zutano and Duke seedlings are commonly used as cold-hardy rootstock. If you're dealing with heavy clay, poor drainage, or water salinity, clonal rootstocks like Dusa or Duke 7 may be worth considering — contact us or reach out to Subtropica Nurseries in Fallbrook for clonal rootstock availability.
